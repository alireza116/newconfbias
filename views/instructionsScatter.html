Instructions

In this study you will encounter charts that represent real data about the relationship between two variables. Each scatter plot displays a number of observations. Each circle in the scatter plot shows the values for the two variables that were measured for one observation. For example, the top right circle in the following chart represents a person who has a very high height and weight. The circle at the bottom left represents a person who has a very low height and very low weight.

Whenever you see a scatter chart with a dataset such as the one above, you should compare the relationship in the observed data to your preexisting belief about the relationship between the two variables.
